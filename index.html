Meet Cheese

Cheese is a 4 year old goldfish won at the Arizona State Fair. Cheese is a household staple and recognizes visitors and will do tricks for food. Alongside Cheese are a snail and a small loach that both help keep the tank clean. Visit the Gallery to view more photos of Cheese and view some Goldfish facts below.

Goldfish are generally split into two types: Fancy goldfish and Common goldfish. Fancy goldfish, or double tail goldfish, are often characterized by a rounder, more stout bodies and are often breed for show. Fancy goldfish can be particularly sensitive to water and tank conditions and can be pricey to purchase. Common goldfish on the other hand have sleeker, more streamlined bodies and are generally more hardy than their purebred cousins. Common goldfish are often referred to as "feeder" fish due to their inexpensive price and wide availability.

Modern goldfish are descendants of carp and were first bred as a food source in East Asia over 1000 years ago. Goldfish breeding was common in ancient China and in the Song Dynasty (AD 960 to 1279) commoners were forbidden from breeding golden or yellow colored goldfish as these were known as “royal fish.” Goldfish have long since been a topic of study, about goldfish Charles Darwin once said, "Passing over an almost infinite diversity of color, we meet with the most extraordinary modifications of structure.” Scientist Shisan C. Chen pioneered the study of modern genetics in China using genetic stocks of goldfish populations that exhibited Mendelian inheritance in goldfish.

Contrary to popular belief goldfish are generally intelligent animals. They possess unique social intelligence and the ability to learn and retain skills such as tricks or tasks. Like dog breeds the type of goldfish breed is known to have an impact on memory and intelligence of a goldfish, Fancy goldfish are much more prone to repetitive and thoughtless behavior which some have hypothesized is due to inbreeding.
